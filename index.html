ðŸ§‰The People There Are SO Nice!ðŸ§‰

The is such a gentle and humble way about many of the people in Argentina. When greeting, you are met with kisses and hugs, and a bright smile that makes you feel accepted and loved. Our family time is also something that we hold dear to our hearts as Argentinians. We value quality-time, and after meals, we have something called a "sobremesa" which is when we stay at the table and talk even after the meal is done. We don't automatically go to wash the dishes and clean the table. We take the time to just be at peace with each other before going on with our day. It is a lovely way to reconnect with those we love! We are passionate about our sports, our cumbia and tango music, our food, and ourselves in our self care and style. We are a community of a country!

ðŸ§‰Don't Even Get Me STARTED On The Food There!ðŸ§‰

I'm not kidding when I say it is so good. Our breakfast usually consists of coffee with cream, and toasted sandwhiches, and faturas, which are pastries like sweet crossants, or types of danishes. Our lunch is usually steak with french fries or fried eggs, milanesa (thin spanish chicken cutlets), and puree de papa (mashed potatoes). and after lunch, we have something at 4-5pm called la merienda, which is like a snacktime, where we also eat faturas, and drink Mate, our national drink. We also have sandwhiches de miga, which are sandwhiches with the crust cut off. For dinner (and we always eat between 8-10), we eat pasta, empanadas, pizza, and our famous Asasos-a spanish styled BBQ with steak and sausages like Chorizo. You literally can't go wrong with ANY of the food there!
